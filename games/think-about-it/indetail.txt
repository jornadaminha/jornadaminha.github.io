"Think About It" - the new PEAK Jackbox game about creating and destroying conspiracies

Round 1: Found Evidence
Players get 2 completely different topics and create a crazy conspiracy. Before all the presentations start, all players get 40 seconds to write down some notes at the same time. After the 40 seconds, the notes are completely hidden, and they are readable only while you're presenting. When presenting, players also get a random image that they have to use in their conspiracy by highlighting an object or something. Presentations have a 45 second hard limit where the host cuts them off after the timer ends (players can also manually end it if they have nothing else). At the end of each one, a random player gets the chance to ask the presenter a "follow-up question" with another 15 second hard limit to both ask and answer. After that, players vote on scales (text-based, not 1-10) for funniness and "could possibly be true?"

Round 2: You're Wrong!
During Round 1, for each conspiracy, one listener will be notified that they will have to prove this conspiracy wrong in THIS round, and then present their OWN version of that conspiracy. Again, they get access to writing notes.

Ending:
After the scores are tallied up, a small awards show is hosted, showing the funniest conspiracies, the most possible conspiracies, and the overall winner of the game.

Few ideas:
The presenter gets a soundboard on their phone with sounds like a vine boom, gasp, audience applause, etc. to use during their presentation, with a maximum of 3 uses.

With 8 players, the calculated playtime is about 18-19 minutes, but realistically it would be 25-30 minutes.
